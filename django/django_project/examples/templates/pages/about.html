About Us

Hope S. Lancaster, Ph.D., is a postdoctoral fellow at Arizona State University in the Schools of Engineering and College of Health Solution. Her doctoral training was completed at Vanderbilt University under the mentorship of Dr. Stephen Camarata. From 2015 to 2017, she was a postdoctoral scholar in the Speech and Hearing Science department at Arizona State University and worked on projects spanning the genetics of childhood apraxia of speech to the effectiveness of early intervention in children with nonsyndromic cleft lip and/or palate. In August 2017, she began working on her research funded by her NIH F32 training grant “Genotype-Phenotype Associations in Reading Disorders” under the joint mentorship of Drs. Jing Li, Shelley Gray, and Valentin Dinu. Her research examines the shared genetic etiology and behavioral symptomology for children with speech sound disorder, developmental language disorder, and/or reading disorders and how understanding these shared traits can improve early identification and intervention. Dr. Lancaster’s background in psychology, statistics, and communication disorders has encouraged her to apply her knowledge of big data and data management to answer translational research questions.
